# Chapter 2: The Night Attack

**OFF CHARLESTON HARBOR, February 17, 1864**  
In a grim first for naval warfare, the Confederate submarine H.L. `Hunley` has sunk the Union warship USS `Housatonic`, proving that an underwater attack craft can destroy a larger enemy vessel in combat. The submarine approached at night, carrying a torpedo charge on a long spar. Lookouts on `Housatonic` saw something coming and opened fire, but the attack still landed.

`Housatonic` went down quickly in the dark waters outside Charleston. Yet the attack's success is shadowed by a second mystery: `Hunley` did not return. The small submarine, powered by men turning a hand crank inside an iron tube, has been lost with all hands.

The larger question raised tonight is unsettling. If one hidden boat can slip through and strike a warship at anchor or on blockade duty, navies will have to think differently about defense. The machine that proved the point also appears to have killed its own crew, a reminder that invention in war often arrives wrapped in danger for both sides.
