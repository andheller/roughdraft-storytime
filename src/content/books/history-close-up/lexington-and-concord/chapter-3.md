# Chapter 3: The Road Back to Boston

The event did not end when the most famous scene was over. British troops search Concord and meet organized resistance at North Bridge. Militia harass the returning column all the way to Boston.

Researchers still handle the details carefully. They often re-check exact first shot is unknowable and participant totals and casualty counts differ among local and military records. Historians usually agree on the broad shape of the event while still debating totals, categories, or which figures best describe what happened.

Memory also shapes this story. "The British are coming" is a later simplification. These were not neat set-piece battles but a chain of clashes.

The Battles of Lexington and Concord mattered because it changed more than the people who stood closest to it. The imperial crisis becomes open war. Local militia show they can fight regular troops in a distributed way. That is why the event remains worth studying long after the original fear, smoke, or confusion have passed.
