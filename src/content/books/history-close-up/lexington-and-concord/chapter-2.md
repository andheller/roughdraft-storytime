# Chapter 2: The Shots at Dawn

**LEXINGTON, MASSACHUSETTS, April 19, 1775**  
Open war has begun in Massachusetts after British regulars marching to seize colonial military stores met armed resistance at Lexington and then faced mounting attacks on the road back from Concord. At dawn on Lexington Green, a brief and still disputed exchange of fire left local militia men dead and the king's troops moving on. By the afternoon, the march had become a running battle.

British forces entered Concord to destroy stores, but colonial militia had already gathered in strength. At the North Bridge, the provincials returned fire and forced regulars back. As the British withdrew toward Boston, men from surrounding towns fired from behind walls, trees, and houses, striking at the column mile after mile.

The importance of the day is clear even while confusion remains over who fired first on the green. Blood has been shed. Colonial resistance is no longer only petitions, speeches, or tea in the harbor. Armed conflict between Britain and its colonies is now a fact.
