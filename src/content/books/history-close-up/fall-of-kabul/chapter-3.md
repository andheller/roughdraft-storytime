# Chapter 3: What the Collapse Left Behind

The event did not end when the most famous scene was over. President Ghani leaves the country. Taliban enter Kabul. Evacuation scenes begin immediately and intensify over the following days at Hamid Karzai International Airport.

Researchers still handle the details carefully. They often re-check provincial collapse timeline, evacuation totals by country and date, and casualty figures from later airport attacks should be separated from the August 15 city takeover itself. Historians usually agree on the broad shape of the event while still debating totals, categories, or which figures best describe what happened.

Memory also shapes this story. This is recent, politically contested history with eyewitness trauma still close. A child-accessible treatment would require careful tone and likely a narrower focus, such as the evacuation or collapse of institutions.

The Fall of Kabul mattered because it changed more than the people who stood closest to it. End of the Islamic Republic of Afghanistan. Collapse of a twenty-year U.S.-backed government. That is why the event remains worth studying long after the original fear, smoke, or confusion have passed.
