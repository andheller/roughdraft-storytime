# Chapter 1: A State Comes Apart

The Fall of Kabul is usually remembered for its most dramatic moment, but the story began earlier. Historians usually date the event to August 15, 2021. The broader Afghan government collapse accelerated across early to mid-August 2021. It unfolded in Kabul, Afghanistan. By the time it reached the point most readers remember, pressure had already been building through fear, planning, rivalry, miscalculation, or stubborn political will.

Among the central figures were Ashraf Ghani, Taliban leadership, Afghan civilians, U.S. and allied evacuation forces. Taliban advances rapidly after U.S. withdrawal timetable and provincial collapses. Afghan security forces disintegrate in many areas faster than expected.

What made the buildup dangerous was the size of the stakes. End of the Islamic Republic of Afghanistan. Collapse of a twenty-year U.S.-backed government.

Anyone watching closely before the decisive turn would have noticed crowds pressing toward airport gates, helicopters over embassy compounds, and families carrying documents, children, and small bags through panic. Those details matter because they show the world before the break, when the outcome was still uncertain but the crisis was already hardening into something larger.
