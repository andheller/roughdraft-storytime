# Chapter 3: Why Stalingrad Changed the War

The event did not end with its most famous scene. What followed mattered just as much, because the aftermath determined how the moment would be remembered and what it would change.

Even when the broad outline is clear, the details still demand care. Historians often agree on the general shape of the event while still debating how best to measure its scale or human cost.

Memory shapes this story as much as documents do. Later retellings can sharpen scenes, simplify motives, or turn messy history into legend.

The Battle of Stalingrad remains worth studying because it changed how later people understood power, danger, and historical turning points. The drama of the moment matters, but its consequences matter more.
