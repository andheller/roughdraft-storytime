# Chapter 2: The Fire Runs Wild

**CHICAGO, October 9, 1871**  
Chicago is burning on a scale that seems to outrun the city itself. What began last night near DeKoven Street has spread with terrifying speed through blocks of wooden buildings, bridges, sidewalks, sheds, and warehouses, driven by wind, drought, and a city built to feed a fire. Sparks and flaming debris have leapt waterways that many assumed would act as barriers.

Firefighters were already strained from a large blaze the previous night, and exhausted crews have struggled to keep pace as the flames moved from one district to another. Residents fled with carts, trunks, and whatever goods they could carry. Many have now lost not only their homes, but all sense of where the fire may stop next.

Rumors about the cause are spreading nearly as quickly as the flames, but the cause itself remains uncertain and the more important fact tonight is structural, not legendary. Chicago's growth, wealth, and building habits made this disaster possible. When the fire finally ends, the city will have to decide what kind of place it will rebuild from the ashes.
