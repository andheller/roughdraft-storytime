# Chapter 3: How Chicago Rebuilt

The event did not end when the most famous scene was over. Flames leap the river and race through the city. The fire burns out after rain and changing conditions. Chicago rebuilds rapidly in a different architectural era.

Researchers still handle the details carefully. They often re-check about 300 dead is common, but exact total is uncertain and structures destroyed and homeless counts vary slightly by source. Historians usually agree on the broad shape of the event while still debating totals, categories, or which figures best describe what happened.

Memory also shapes this story. The cow-kicking-the-lantern story is probably false and was unfairly pinned on the O'Learys. Multiple Great Fires occurred across the region in 1871; unusual weather mattered.

The Great Chicago Fire mattered because it changed more than the people who stood closest to it. Urban vulnerability in a fast-growing industrial city. Disaster becomes a turning point in city planning and rebuilding. That is why the event remains worth studying long after the original fear, smoke, or confusion have passed.
