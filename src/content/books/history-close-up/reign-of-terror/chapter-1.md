# Chapter 1: A Republic Under Pressure

The Reign of Terror is usually remembered for its most dramatic moment, but the story began earlier. Historians usually date the event to Commonly dated from September 5, 1793 to July 27, 1794 (9 Thermidor Year II). It unfolded in Revolutionary France, centered in Paris but not limited to it. By the time it reached the point most readers remember, pressure had already been building through fear, planning, rivalry, miscalculation, or stubborn political will.

Among the central figures were Maximilien Robespierre, Georges Danton, Jean-Paul Marat, Louis Antoine de Saint-Just, Committee of Public Safety. France faces foreign war, internal revolt, and political fear. Revolutionary government expands emergency powers.

What made the buildup dangerous was the size of the stakes. Survival of the revolution as leaders understood it. Struggle over whether emergency violence preserves liberty or destroys it.

Anyone watching closely before the decisive turn would have noticed daily carts to the guillotine, packed galleries at political trials, and whisper networks of denunciation and fear. Those details matter because they show the world before the break, when the outcome was still uncertain but the crisis was already hardening into something larger.
