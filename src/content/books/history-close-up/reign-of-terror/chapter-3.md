# Chapter 3: How the Terror Consumed the Revolution

The event did not end when the most famous scene was over. Revolutionary tribunals accelerate prosecutions. Executions by guillotine become central symbols of political purification. Robespierre and allies fall in Thermidor, ending the high phase of the Terror.

Researchers still handle the details carefully. They often re-check executed by tribunal vs killed in civil war and repression are different categories and vendee deaths should not be casually merged into Paris guillotine counts. Historians usually agree on the broad shape of the event while still debating totals, categories, or which figures best describe what happened.

Memory also shapes this story. "Terror" was not one single machine; local enforcement varied. Avoid framing only as madness; it emerged from war, ideology, and power struggle.

The Reign of Terror mattered because it changed more than the people who stood closest to it. Survival of the revolution as leaders understood it. Struggle over whether emergency violence preserves liberty or destroys it. That is why the event remains worth studying long after the original fear, smoke, or confusion have passed.
