# Chapter 2: Fire in the Channel

**OFF THE ENGLISH COAST, August 8, 1588**  
Spain's great Armada, sent to secure control of the Channel and support an invasion of England, has been thrown into disorder after English attacks and a night of alarm off Calais. Fire ships sent drifting into the anchored Spanish fleet forced captains to cut cables and scatter. Today, English ships pressed their advantage at Gravelines, harrying the larger fleet before it could regain full order.

The Armada had entered the Channel as the centerpiece of King Philip II's attempt to crush England and restore Catholic power. Yet the campaign has revealed how difficult that plan was even before battle began. Coordination with invasion forces in the Spanish Netherlands has failed, and the English have used range, maneuver, and relentless pressure to avoid the kind of close action Spain wanted.

Weather now threatens to do what battle began. Driven north and unable to return safely through the Channel on its original plan, the fleet faces a long and dangerous retreat around Scotland and Ireland. This does not end Spanish power, but it does mean England has survived the invasion scare of the summer.
