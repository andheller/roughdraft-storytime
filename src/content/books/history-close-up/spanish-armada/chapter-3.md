# Chapter 3: Why the Armada Failed

The event did not end when the most famous scene was over. Fire ships at Calais disrupt Spanish formation. Battle of Gravelines drives the Armada north. Storms and navigational trouble shatter the retreat around Scotland and Ireland.

Researchers still handle the details carefully. They often re-check number of ships in the Armada is usually around 130 but depends on inclusion rules and loss totals differ by whether combat, wrecks, and later attrition are combined. Historians usually agree on the broad shape of the event while still debating totals, categories, or which figures best describe what happened.

Memory also shapes this story. Weather mattered, but English tactics and Spanish strategic problems mattered too. The Armada's failure did not instantly end Spanish power.

The Spanish Armada mattered because it changed more than the people who stood closest to it. Threat of invasion of Protestant England. Imperial, religious, and naval rivalry. That is why the event remains worth studying long after the original fear, smoke, or confusion have passed.
