# Chapter 1: The Straits and the Plan

The Gallipoli Campaign is usually remembered for its most dramatic moment, but the story began earlier. Historians usually date the event to Naval phase begins February 19, 1915; landings on April 25, 1915; final evacuation completed by January 9, 1916. It unfolded in Gallipoli Peninsula and Dardanelles, Ottoman Empire, now Turkey. By the time it reached the point most readers remember, pressure had already been building through fear, planning, rivalry, miscalculation, or stubborn political will.

Among the central figures were Winston Churchill in strategic background, Ian Hamilton, Mustafa Kemal, AN.

What made the buildup dangerous was that the people inside the story were already moving toward a confrontation that would be hard to reverse.

By the opening of the crisis, the event had already developed its own momentum. The final turning point would feel sudden, but it was built from many earlier choices.
