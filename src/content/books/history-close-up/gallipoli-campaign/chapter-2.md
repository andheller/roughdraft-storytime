# Chapter 2: The Landings Under Fire

**GALLIPOLI PENINSULA, April 25, 1915**  
Allied forces attempting to force the Dardanelles and strike a major blow against the Ottoman Empire have gone ashore today on the Gallipoli Peninsula under fierce fire and in punishing terrain. Beaches that looked manageable from offshore quickly gave way to steep ridges, scrub, confusion, and determined defenders shooting from above.

The operation aims at something enormous: opening the straits, threatening Constantinople, and changing the course of the wider war. But strategy on paper has met cliffs, bad landings, and the stubborn realities of combat. Troops from Britain, France, Australia, New Zealand, and elsewhere are now digging in where they can, often only yards from Ottoman lines.

If the campaign continues in this shape, it may become less a rapid breakthrough than a grinding contest of trenches, thirst, disease, and attrition. Today's landings show both the ambition of the Allied plan and the cost that may follow if it stalls.
