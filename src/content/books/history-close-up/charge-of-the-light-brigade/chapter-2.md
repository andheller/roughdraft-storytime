# Chapter 2: Into the Valley

**BALACLAVA, CRIMEA, October 25, 1854**  
A British cavalry charge of astonishing courage and terrible cost has left the battlefield at Balaclava stunned and angry after the Light Brigade rode straight into a valley swept by Russian guns. The order that sent them forward appears to have been misunderstood, disputed, or badly conveyed, but the result is not in doubt. Men and horses were torn apart under fire from the front and flanks.

The charge took place during a battle already marked by confusion over captured guns and shifting positions. Lord Raglan, observing from a height, seems to have intended one thing. The commanders lower down understood another. Lord Lucan and Lord Cardigan sent the brigade on, and Captain Nolan, who carried the order, was killed early in the action.

The survivors reached the guns and somehow returned, but the exploit will be remembered less as a success than as a symbol of what happens when bravery is wasted by failure in command. The men did what was asked. That is precisely why the disaster is so hard to defend.
