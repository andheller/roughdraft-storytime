# Chapter 3: How Protest Became Crisis

The event did not end when the most famous scene was over. Hutchinson refuses clearance without unloading. Men disguised in part as Mohawks board the ships and dump tea into the harbor. Britain responds with the Coercive or Intolerable Acts.

Researchers still handle the details carefully. They often re-check number of tea chests is usually 342 and value of destroyed tea should be presented with period and modern context carefully. Historians usually agree on the broad shape of the event while still debating totals, categories, or which figures best describe what happened.

Memory also shapes this story. Not all Bostonians supported destruction of private property. Mohawk disguise has symbolic and political meaning; avoid cartoon treatment.

The Boston Tea Party mattered because it changed more than the people who stood closest to it. Direct challenge to parliamentary taxation and imperial authority. Escalation from protest to punitive imperial response. That is why the event remains worth studying long after the original fear, smoke, or confusion have passed.
