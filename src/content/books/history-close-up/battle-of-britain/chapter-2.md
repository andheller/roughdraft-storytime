# Chapter 2: The Day the Raids Peaked

**LONDON, September 15, 1940**  
Britain's fight for the sky reached a fierce new peak today as German aircraft crossed the Channel in force and Royal Air Force squadrons rose again to meet them over southern England and London. For weeks the Luftwaffe has tried to break Britain's air defenses. This morning, as sirens sounded and control rooms tracked incoming raids, fighter pilots were sent up in wave after wave to stop the attack.

The battle now underway is about far more than a day's bombing. Since July, Germany has been trying to destroy RAF Fighter Command and clear the way for a possible invasion. Radar stations, ground observers, telephones, plotting tables, and exhausted ground crews have become as important to Britain's defense as the pilots in the air. Hurricanes have carried much of the burden, while Spitfires and other aircraft join the struggle wherever they can.

Londoners watched contrails twist overhead while smoke rose from damaged districts below. The outcome of the campaign is still uncertain, but Britain has not broken. If the RAF can keep flying, the German plan to force invasion or surrender may yet fail.
