# Chapter 3: Why the Bastille Mattered

The event did not end when the most famous scene was over. They move on the Bastille fortress-prison. Negotiation collapses into fighting. Governor de Launay surrenders; he is seized and killed by the crowd.

Researchers still handle the details carefully. They often re-check only seven prisoners were inside when the Bastille fell, which surprises many readers and death toll estimates vary. Historians usually agree on the broad shape of the event while still debating totals, categories, or which figures best describe what happened.

Memory also shapes this story. The Bastille mattered more as symbol and arsenal than as a crowded political prison in that moment. Violence by the crowd should not be erased.

The Storming of the Bastille mattered because it changed more than the people who stood closest to it. Symbolic blow against royal absolutism. Paris crowds show that armed popular action can change national politics. That is why the event remains worth studying long after the original fear, smoke, or confusion have passed.
