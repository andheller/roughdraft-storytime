# Chapter 1: Paris Before the Storm

The Storming of the Bastille is usually remembered for its most dramatic moment, but the story began earlier. Historians usually date the event to July 14, 1789. It unfolded in Paris, France. By the time it reached the point most readers remember, pressure had already been building through fear, planning, rivalry, miscalculation, or stubborn political will.

Among the central figures were Bernard-Rene de Launay, Paris crowd leaders, French Guards defectors, Louis XVI in the background as monarch. Paris is tense after dismissal of Necker and fear of royal repression. Crowds seize weapons from the Invalides but need gunpowder.

What made the buildup dangerous was the size of the stakes. Symbolic blow against royal absolutism. Paris crowds show that armed popular action can change national politics.

Anyone watching closely before the decisive turn would have noticed crowds dragging cannon through Paris streets, smoke and musket fire around stone towers and drawbridges, and heads on pikes in the aftermath. Those details matter because they show the world before the break, when the outcome was still uncertain but the crisis was already hardening into something larger.
