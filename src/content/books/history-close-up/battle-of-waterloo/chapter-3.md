# Chapter 3: The End of the Gamble

The event did not end when the most famous scene was over. Fighting at Quatre Bras and Ligny sets the stage. At Waterloo, Wellington holds defensive ridges and strongpoints. Prussian arrival late in the day helps break the French army.

Researchers still handle the details carefully. They often re-check total troop strengths vary depending on timing and units counted and casualty estimates differ by national history tradition. Historians usually agree on the broad shape of the event while still debating totals, categories, or which figures best describe what happened.

Memory also shapes this story. Waterloo was not just Wellington vs Napoleon; the Prussian role was decisive. Heroic legend often simplifies command failures and battlefield confusion.

The Battle of Waterloo mattered because it changed more than the people who stood closest to it. Final attempt by Napoleon to reclaim European dominance. Allied victory decides the Hundred Days. That is why the event remains worth studying long after the original fear, smoke, or confusion have passed.
