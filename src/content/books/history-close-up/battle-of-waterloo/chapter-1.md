# Chapter 1: Napoleon Returns

The Battle of Waterloo is usually remembered for its most dramatic moment, but the story began earlier. Historians usually date the event to June 18, 1815. It unfolded in Near Waterloo, in present-day Belgium. By the time it reached the point most readers remember, pressure had already been building through fear, planning, rivalry, miscalculation, or stubborn political will.

Among the central figures were Napoleon Bonaparte, Duke of Wellington, Gebhard Leberecht von Blucher. Napoleon returns from Elba and rebuilds his rule. He moves quickly to defeat allied armies before they can unite.

What made the buildup dangerous was the size of the stakes. Final attempt by Napoleon to reclaim European dominance. Allied victory decides the Hundred Days.

Anyone watching closely before the decisive turn would have noticed mud-slowed artillery after heavy rain, hougoumont in flames while fighting rages around its walls, and repeated cavalry charges against infantry squares. Those details matter because they show the world before the break, when the outcome was still uncertain but the crisis was already hardening into something larger.
