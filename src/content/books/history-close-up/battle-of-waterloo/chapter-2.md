# Chapter 2: The Battle in the Mud

**NEAR WATERLOO, June 18, 1815**  
Napoleon Bonaparte's final bid to regain mastery of Europe has ended in defeat here after a long day of mud, smoke, and mounting pressure from two enemy armies. The French attacked the Duke of Wellington's line again and again across a rain-soaked battlefield, but the defense held long enough for Prussian forces under Blucher to arrive in growing strength on Napoleon's flank.

Farmhouses such as Hougoumont and La Haye Sainte became small fortresses inside the larger battle. Late in the day, Napoleon committed the Imperial Guard, hoping one last assault would break the allied center. Instead, the attack failed, and the French army's morale cracked with it.

Waterloo closes the dramatic return that began when Napoleon escaped from Elba and marched back to power in France. It also appears to close his political future for good. This was not Wellington's victory alone. Without the Prussians, the story might have ended differently. But tonight one conclusion is certain: Napoleon's era as ruler of Europe is over.
