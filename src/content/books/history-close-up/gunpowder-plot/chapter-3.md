# Chapter 3: The Plot in Memory

The event did not end when the most famous scene was over. A warning letter reaches authorities. Searchers find Guy Fawkes guarding the explosives. Plotters flee; several are killed or captured; survivors are tried and executed.

Researchers still handle the details carefully. They often re-check number of barrels is often given as 36, but sources sometimes differ in how they describe the stockpile and intended blast radius is often dramatized. Historians usually agree on the broad shape of the event while still debating totals, categories, or which figures best describe what happened.

Memory also shapes this story. Anti-Catholic propaganda shaped the memory of the plot. Guy Fawkes was not the mastermind; Catesby drove the conspiracy.

The Gunpowder Plot mattered because it changed more than the people who stood closest to it. Intended decapitation strike against king, lords, and commons. Fear of religious violence in post-Reformation England. That is why the event remains worth studying long after the original fear, smoke, or confusion have passed.
