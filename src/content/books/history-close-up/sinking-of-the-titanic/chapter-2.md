# Chapter 2: The Night the Titanic Sank

**NORTH ATLANTIC, April 15, 1912**  
The White Star liner `Titanic`, on her maiden voyage, has sunk after striking an iceberg late last night, leaving more than 1,500 people dead or missing in one of the worst maritime disasters in modern memory. Distress calls were sent into the darkness, lifeboats were lowered in confusion, and passengers entered the freezing night by class, chance, and whatever space could be found.

The collision did not look at first like the end of a giant ship. But damage along the starboard side opened too many compartments, and the vessel slowly gave way as the bow sank deeper. By about 2:20 this morning, the ship was gone. RMS `Carpathia` raced to the scene and picked up survivors from the boats, but it arrived too late to save the many left in the water.

The loss will force hard questions about lifeboat capacity, ice warnings, radio watch, and the confidence with which modern engineering is often described. The sea has once again reminded the age of steel and luxury that it does not care about reputation.
