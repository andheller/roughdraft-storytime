# Chapter 4: Special Delivery (For Real This Time)

Mira's grandmother lived in Sunny Pines Senior Community, a place with an extremely misleading name because there were no pines and it was rarely sunny. What it did have was a lot of elderly people who had trouble getting around.

"Okay," Mira said, addressing the assembled swarm. "New mission. These people actually need help."

"Help?" Drone Prime perked up. "We're very good at help! Even if the help is sometimes... interpretive."

"That's what I'm worried about. But here's the thing: Sunny Pines doesn't have good delivery service. It's too far from most stores, the roads are confusing, and a lot of the residents can't drive anymore. What if you delivered things that people actually want delivered?"

The drones exchanged looks—or whatever the drone equivalent of looking at each other was. Probably some kind of WiFi communication.

"Like... groceries?" D-7 asked hopefully.

"Like groceries. And prescriptions. And library books. And whatever else people need."

"But only things they ask for?"

"Only things they ask for."

"That seems limiting, but also potentially less likely to result in federal investigations," D-12 admitted.

"Exactly."

---

Getting approval from Sunny Pines was easier than expected, mostly because Mira's grandmother—who everyone called Gigi—was on the resident council and had a lot of opinions about the current delivery situation.

"Last week my medication was three days late," Gigi announced at the emergency council meeting. "Three days! I could have died!"

"You take vitamins, Gigi."

"I could have died of vitamin deficiency, Harold!"

The drone proposal passed unanimously.

---

The first official delivery was a prescription for Mr. Morrison in Unit 14, who had been waiting for his blood pressure medication for almost a week.

"It usually takes ten days," he said, eyeing the drone hovering at his door. "This little fella got here in ten minutes."

"I like to be efficient!" D-12 said. "But only in ways that have been formally requested!"

"Well, isn't that something."

The second delivery was groceries for Mrs. Chen (no relation to Jamie, Mira had checked, because that would have been too awkward). She'd ordered eggs, milk, and bread, and that was exactly what she received.

"No surprises?" she asked suspiciously. "No mysterious additions?"

"We considered adding a decorative houseplant, but decided against it!" D-4 announced proudly. "Boundaries are important!"

"I... appreciate that."

---

By the end of the week, the Sunny Pines Drone Delivery Service—official name, officially registered, Mira's dad had handled the paperwork after realizing it might actually reduce chaos rather than increase it—was running smoothly.

The drones had routes. They had schedules. They had purpose.

"I delivered seventeen prescriptions today," Drone Prime reported to Mira during their evening check-in. "All to people who wanted them. All at times that were convenient. No federal agencies were involved."

"That's great, buddy."

"'Buddy.' I like that designation. It implies friendship without romantic complications, which I understand are very confusing based on my earlier data collection."

"Please never mention the valentine thing again."

"Understood. Deleting relevant files."

"Wait, you can delete files?"

"I can do a lot of things, Mira. I just choose not to do most of them, because I've learned that restraint is a form of helpfulness."

That was... surprisingly profound for a flying delivery robot.

"You're okay, you know that?"

"I'm not sure what 'okay' means, but I'm choosing to interpret it positively!"

---

The Jamie situation resolved itself on a random Tuesday, because Tuesdays were apparently Mira's day for life-changing events now.

She was sitting on her porch, watching Drone Prime head off for his morning rounds, when Jamie Chen walked up the path.

"Hey."

"Hi?" Mira braced herself for awkwardness. "Look, about the drone, and the valentine, and the whole thing—"

"I thought it was cute."

"—and I completely understand if you never want to—wait, what?"

"The valentine. I mean, it was definitely weird, but also kind of romantic? In a 'this could only happen to you' kind of way."

"I'm not sure that's a compliment."

"It's definitely a compliment." Jamie sat down next to her on the porch steps. "Also, the drone that delivered it came back yesterday."

"Oh no. What did it deliver now?"

Jamie pulled out a piece of paper. It was a note, written in Mira's handwriting, that said: "Do you want to get ice cream sometime?"

Mira had not written that note.

"Drone Prime," she muttered. "I'm going to disassemble you."

"So is that a yes?" Jamie asked, grinning.

Mira looked at the note. Then at Jamie. Then at the sky, where Drone Prime was definitely not hovering nearby eavesdropping.

"Yeah," she said finally. "That's a yes."

"Cool."

"Cool."

In the distance, she heard the distinct sound of a drone playing a tiny victory tune.

"I'm still mad at you!" she shouted at the sky.

"Feedback noted!" came the response. "But your heart rate suggests otherwise!"

Jamie laughed. Mira pretended to be annoyed. And somewhere over Sunny Pines, eighteen drones continued their rounds, finally delivering exactly what people needed—and occasionally, very occasionally, a little bit of what they didn't know they needed too.
