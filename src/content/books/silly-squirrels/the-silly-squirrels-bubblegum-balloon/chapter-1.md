# Chapter 1: The Festival Needs Wow

The annual Nutville Kite Festival had one problem.

It had plenty of kites.
Beautiful kites. Silly kites. One kite shaped like a pear wearing spectacles.

What it did not have, according to Mayor Buttersworth, was wow.

"We need a grand finale!" he declared at the planning meeting. "A marvel! A spectacle! Something that makes the whole town point upward and say, 'Oh my acorns!'"

Nutty's ears shot up instantly.

Point upward? Spectacle?
That was invitation-level language.

Mama Nutwobble was already bouncing in her chair. "We could make the biggest kite ever!"

Papa Nutwobble raised a thoughtful paw. "Or a kite that serves snacks."

Grandma Nutwobble looked at both of them and said, "No flames."

Nobody had said anything about flames, which was not comforting.

Nutty flipped open his notebook and drew a giant round balloon above the festival field. Then he added a basket, a banner, and decorative streamers that looked suspiciously impractical.

"A hot-air balloon," he said. "But not just any balloon. A bubblegum-pink balloon! A floating festival balloon! A sky parade!"

Tony the Delivery Fox blinked. "Can you build a balloon?"

Nutty nodded the bold nod of someone who had not yet encountered balloon reality.
"Of course. It is basically a basket with ambition."

Grandma sighed into her tea.
"That sentence alone should stop us."

It did not stop anyone.

By the end of the meeting, the whole plan had grown ribbons, flags, a brass horn, and something Papa kept calling the Aerial Snack Shelf.

The Nutville Kite Festival was about to become much more airborne than anyone sensible preferred.
