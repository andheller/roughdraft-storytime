# Chapter 2: A Balloon the Color of Dessert

They built the Bubblegum Balloon in the meadow behind the mill.

The balloon itself was stitched from bright pink fabric panels donated by half the town. The basket came from an old produce crate reinforced with ropes, braces, and Grandma's repeated warning that the sky was not a place for shortcuts.

Mama painted the name across the side:

**THE PINK PUFFER**

Papa added a tiny shelf for crackers.
Grandma removed the shelf.
Papa added a smaller shelf.
Grandma pretended not to see it.

Nutty supervised the burner, the ropes, and the steering fins.

"Do steering fins steer?" asked Tony.

Nutty hesitated.

"They certainly encourage steering," he said.

Nobody loved that answer.

Still, when the balloon began to fill, it was magnificent.
The pink fabric billowed upward like a giant candy cloud.
The basket creaked nobly. The ropes tightened. The whole meadow glowed with sunset light and bad judgment.

Mrs. Hedgehog arrived with a clipboard and inspected the basket.
"How many passengers?"

"Four," said Nutty.

"How many snacks?" Papa asked.

"That was not my question," said Mrs. Hedgehog.

By evening, the balloon stood ready. The whole town gathered around it with open mouths and hopeful faces.
Even Grandma had to admit it looked impressive.

She looked at Nutty.
"Remember: a balloon is not fast. A balloon is not clever. A balloon goes where the air argues."

Nutty grinned. "Then we'll argue politely back."

Grandma stared at him for one full second.
"That is not how wind works."
