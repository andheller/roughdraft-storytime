# Chapter 2: Sorting with Extra Gears

The Mailbox Mix-Up Machine took over the whole post office lobby.

Nutty built a conveyor belt from old rollers.
Mama installed flippy paddles that knocked letters left or right.
Papa added a basket rack and a sign that said:

**POST RESPONSIBLY**

Then underneath, in smaller letters:

**NO SOUP**

Tony looked at the machine nervously. "Has anyone mailed soup before?"

Papa nodded. "This is a creative town."

The machine had slots for:

- Creek Road
- Orchard Hill
- Town Square
- Mystery Barn Area
- Urgent-ish
- Definitely Grandma

Grandma pointed at the last label.
"Remove that."

Nutty removed it.
Then added a perfectly ordinary basket in the same spot and looked innocent.

They fed the first stack of letters into the top tray.
The rollers grabbed them. The paddles flipped. The chutes rattled. Envelopes zipped into baskets with cheerful little thwips.

At first it worked beautifully.
Creek Road mail went to Creek Road.
Town Square mail landed in the right basket.
A postcard from the beach arrived exactly where it belonged, which was to Aunt Mabel's porch swing and nowhere else.

Tony stared in amazement. "It's sorting!"

Nutty puffed up proudly. "Of course it's sorting. It is very advanced."

Then Papa fed in a glossy magazine, two birthday cards, and one thick bill all at the same time.

The machine made a noise like a cough wearing gears.
One paddle stuck. Another spun too fast. A chute shivered. And suddenly the birthday cards shot into the Orchard Hill basket while the bill landed in the Mystery Barn Area and the magazine disappeared entirely.

"Where did it go?" Tony asked.

A moment later, the magazine popped out of the back of the machine and slapped Mayor Buttersworth right on the hat.

"Remarkable efficiency," said the mayor, though he did not sound convinced.

Nutty scribbled in his notebook.
"Important update: magazines are ambitious."
