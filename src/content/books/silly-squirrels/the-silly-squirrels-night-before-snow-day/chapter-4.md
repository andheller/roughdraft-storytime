# Chapter 4: The Great Midnight Sled Rescue

By midnight, Nutville had two separate emergencies.

First, the Snowstarter 3000 was still making snow.
Second, the town's best sleds had blown away in the growing white gusts and were now sliding down Maple Lane without riders.

"We need those sleds!" cried Mama. "One of them is my fast one."

Papa tied on his boots and wrapped himself in three scarves. "This has become a rescue mission."

They formed a team.
Mama would chase the sleds.
Papa would carry cocoa for morale.
Tony would shine a lantern.
Mrs. Hedgehog would make a list of what had gone wrong.
Grandma would handle the machine.
Nutty would do whatever Grandma told him immediately.

This was a new role for him.

They plunged into the snow.
One sled was stuck in a shrub.
One had wedged under the bakery steps.
One was circling the fountain for reasons nobody understood.
And Mama's fastest sled was gliding majestically toward the pond.

"Not the pond!" Mama shouted, sprinting after it.

Meanwhile, back at the shed, Grandma pulled open the machine's side panel.
Inside, everything was coated in icy fluff.
The seasonal bell was ringing nonstop.
A little sign popped up from a slot and read:

**WINTER ACHIEVED. CONTINUING ANYWAY.**

Grandma handed Nutty a wrench.
"You built the snow loop backward."

"There is a snow loop?"

"There is now. Hold this."

Together they tightened bolts, untangled the fan belt, and unplugged the ice-crusher from the bell, which nobody could explain but definitely seemed unwise.

Out by the pond, Mama made a flying leap and landed on her runaway sled just before it reached the icy bank.
She whooped all the way back up the lane carrying two extra sleds behind her like captured fish.

"Got them!" she shouted.

At that exact moment, Grandma hit the final valve with the wrench.

The Snowstarter 3000 sneezed one last giant puff of snow straight upward.
It looked like a white mushroom cloud wearing mittens.

Then it stopped.

The night went quiet.

Soft flakes drifted down.
Real flakes.
Ordinary flakes.
Natural flakes.

Everyone looked up.

Papa blinked. "The actual snow finally arrived?"

Grandma nodded. "Winter likes an audience."
