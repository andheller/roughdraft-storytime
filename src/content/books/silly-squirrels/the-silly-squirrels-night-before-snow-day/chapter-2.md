# Chapter 2: The Snowstarter 3000

The Snowstarter 3000 was built in the shed behind the oak tree.

Nutty said this was because the shed had good airflow.
Grandma said it was because she did not want the contraption near the curtains.

By bedtime, the machine included:

- two fans
- one ice-crusher borrowed from the lemonade stand
- a barrel of very cold pond water
- a glittering pipe system
- four soup strainers
- a crank labeled **MORE WINTER**
- and a bell that rang whenever the machine felt "seasonal"

"Machines do not have feelings," said Mrs. Hedgehog.

The bell rang.

"This one seems confident," said Tony.

Mama tugged on her mittens. "Let's make snow!"

Nutty climbed onto the stool and tapped the main gauge. "The fans will spin the cold air, the crusher will fluff the ice, the water mist will freeze, and BOOM! Backyard snow."

Grandma stared.
"You said boom much too casually."

Papa carried out a tray of cocoa. "Before we begin, has anyone asked whether the sky objects to competition?"

No one had.

Nutty pulled the starter cord.

The Snowstarter 3000 coughed.
Then it rattled.
Then the fans spun so fast Papa's scarf stood straight out behind him.
The bell rang twelve times in a row.
The soup strainers trembled heroically.

For one magical second, tiny white flakes drifted from the pipe.

Nutty gasped. "It's working!"

Then the left fan sneezed.
The right pipe burped.
And a thick puff of snow blasted sideways into the woodshed, covering half the firewood and one deeply offended raccoon.

"Good start!" Nutty shouted.

"That was not a start," said Grandma. "That was a warning."
