# Chapter 1: Harvest Hoopla Trouble

Every autumn, Nutville held the Harvest Hoopla Parade.

There were marching acorn drummers, leaf confetti tossers, synchronized wagon twirlers, and at least one goose who joined every year without permission.

This year, Mayor Buttersworth wanted the biggest parade float in town history.

"Something memorable!" he declared at the planning meeting. "Something festive! Something that says *Nutville is organized and definitely not sticky!*"

"Why would it be sticky?" asked Tony the Delivery Fox.

The mayor looked away. "No reason from last year."

Nutty's eyes shone. He loved any sentence that included the words biggest, history, or definitely.

"We'll build the float!" he shouted.

Mama Nutwobble leaped onto a barrel. "Fast float!"

Papa Nutwobble raised a paw. "Comfortable float. With snacks."

Nutty grabbed the chalk and drew a rectangle on the town hall board.
Then he added wheels.
Then music pipes.
Then a little chimney.
Then what looked suspiciously like a giant frying pan.

Mrs. Hedgehog adjusted her glasses. "Why does the parade float appear to be cooking breakfast?"

Nutty turned dramatically. "Because ordinary floats are BORING. This float will celebrate harvest, friendship, and pancakes."

The room went quiet.

Papa's voice trembled. "Parade pancakes?"

"Parade pancakes," Nutty repeated.

Mama gasped happily. Tony gasped nervously. Grandma Nutwobble, who had been knitting in the back row, narrowed one eye the way she always did when a plan became funny enough to be dangerous.

Mayor Buttersworth considered the sketch. "Will the pancakes stay on the float?"

Nutty hesitated for one tiny moment too long.

"Mostly," he said.

That afternoon they rolled into the old mill workshop and began construction.

The float would have:

- four oversized wagon wheels
- a giant smiling acorn on the front
- brass music horns that played parade tunes
- side banners that said **HOOPLA!**
- and a pancake flipper powered by pedals, springs, and confidence

"No syrup cannon," said Mrs. Hedgehog firmly.

Nutty crossed out one line on the plans.

"Fine," he said. "No *large* syrup cannon."

Grandma looked up from her knitting. "I heard that."
