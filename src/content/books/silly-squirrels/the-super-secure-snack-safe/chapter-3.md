# Chapter 3: Passwords, Panic, and Pasta Lasers

The next morning, Papa wanted one cracker.

Just one.

Not a whole snack.

Not an emergency ration.

Just one small crunchy friend to go with his tea.

Twenty-three minutes later, he was still on Security Step Nine.

"State your purpose," boomed the safe.

"I would like a cracker," Papa said through clenched teeth.

"STATE YOUR PURPOSE WITH MORE CONFIDENCE."

Papa puffed up his chest. "I WOULD LIKE A CRACKER!"

"SUSPICIOUSLY LOUD. PROCEED TO SECURITY QUESTION."

The whole family gathered around.

"Security question one," said the safe. "Name your first imaginary squirrel."

Papa blinked. "What?"

Nutty flipped through his notes. "Ah. Anti-robot test."

"Why would a robot want my crackers?"

"Papa, please focus."

Papa groaned. "Captain Nibbles."

"Accepted," said the safe. "Security question two. Are you now or have you ever been a goose?"

"NO!"

"Please answer in complete sentence."

Papa shut his eyes. "I have never been a goose."

"Good," said the safe. "Geese know what they did."

Tony, who had stopped by with the morning mail, slowly backed toward the door. "I don't think your safe is normal."

"Normal is how thieves win," Nutty replied.

Finally, after another password, two tail scans, one walnut dial spin, and a suspiciously musical sneeze test, the safe clicked open.

Papa reached inside with tears of relief in his eyes.

Then the safe snapped shut again.

"CRACKER REMOVAL TOO GREEDY," it said.

Papa looked down at his paw.

He was holding three crackers.

"I got excited!"

By noon, the safe had created seventeen separate small disasters.

Mrs. Hedgehog stopped by to borrow cinnamon for muffins and triggered the flour cannon so hard she emerged looking like an angry powdered donut.

Mayor Buttersworth forgot the password halfway through saying it and got trapped in the Spaghetti Verification Corridor for eleven minutes.

Tony tried to deliver a parcel and was interrogated by the horn.

"Package contents?" blared the safe.

"Socks," Tony answered.

"TOO VAGUE."

"Blue socks!"

"STILL SHADY."

Mama was the only one who almost enjoyed it.

"I like the challenge," she said, crab-walking through the spaghetti beams with a muffin in her mouth.

"You set off seven alarms on purpose," Nutty told her.

"Because they sound neat together."

And worst of all, despite all the locks and horns and flour and pasta lasers, the snacks kept disappearing.

Every morning Papa unlocked the safe using the now-ridiculous password:

`SUPREME-EMERGENCY-SNACK-COMMANDER-ALPHA-CRUNCH-DELUXE-PRO-MAX`

And every morning something was missing.

One day it was the pretzel twists.

The next day it was half the cinnamon acorns.

On Thursday, an entire emergency cookie roll vanished, leaving behind nothing but three crumbs and what looked like a very sleepy smudge.

Papa clutched the safe with both paws. "This is impossible."

Nutty nodded grimly. "Agreed. The safe is too secure for ordinary theft."

"Which means," Mama said, leaning forward, "we are dealing with an EXTRA-ordinary thief."

Papa gasped. "A master criminal."

Tony lowered his voice. "A phantom snack bandit."

Mayor Buttersworth put on sunglasses indoors. "A criminal genius."

Grandma, who was eating peanuts in the corner, said, "Or one of you is still being silly."

Nobody listened because the mystery had become too exciting.

Nutty pulled down the workshop map of Nutville and pinned a large note to the middle:

**THE CASE OF THE IMPOSSIBLE SNACK BANDIT**

He listed the clues:

- No broken locks
- No bent hinges
- No forced entry
- Crumbs inside safe
- Sleepy smudge on shelf
- Papa becoming strangely hungry at breakfast
- Blue fuzz on the latch
- A delivery slip under the workbench
- Cocoa pawprints near the rafters

Papa lifted a paw. "That last clue feels personal."

Nutty circled it anyway.

"Hold on," said Tony. "Why is there a delivery slip on the suspect board?"

"Because it says you were here at six-oh-three yesterday morning," Nutty replied.

Tony threw up his paws. "I was delivering investigation muffins. Papa ordered them labeled `for morale.`"

Mayor Buttersworth peered at the board. "And why is there blue fuzz?"

Everyone looked at his sash.

"This is ceremonial blue," the mayor said quickly. "Completely different from suspicious blue."

Mama lifted both paws. Cocoa dust puffed into the air.

"And before anyone asks," she said, "yes, I did run along the rafters this morning. The lasers looked lonely."

Mrs. Hedgehog wrote on her clipboard for a long moment.

"Conclusion?" Nutty asked.

"Annoyingly," she said, "all of you remain excellent suspects."

For the next hour Nutty conducted official interviews.

Tony admitted he had come by at dawn three times that week with deliveries and once with muffins.

Mayor Buttersworth admitted he had tried the dial "only a little" because he wanted to know whether the future acorn bank would feel official enough.

Mama admitted she had deliberately set off two alarms and "possibly one decorative whistle" while testing the spaghetti beams.

Each explanation made sense.

Each explanation also sounded terrible.

The mystery grew boots and started tap-dancing.

By sunset, the investigation was in full swing.

Mama dusted the floor with cocoa powder to catch footprints.

Tony hid behind a barrel wearing a fake mustache for detective reasons.

Mrs. Hedgehog set up a clue table with labeled jars.

Mayor Buttersworth insisted on a code name and chose `Eagle Seven`, even though he was not an eagle and there was no `Eagle One` through `Eagle Six`.

Papa sat in a chair by the safe holding a flashlight, a notebook, and a sandwich he had promised not to eat unless things got "investigatively intense."

At exactly midnight, everybody froze.

The workshop was quiet.

The safe sat gleaming in the moonlight.

The cocoa powder remained smooth.

The bells were still.

The spaghetti lasers glowed softly.

Then, from the far corner of the room, came a sound.

Not a footstep.

Not a creak.

Not a whisper.

A snore.

Everyone slowly turned.

Papa was asleep in his chair.

His chin had dropped to his chest.

His paws were folded over his sandwich.

And in his sleep, very softly but very clearly, he mumbled:

"Emergency... crunchy support..."

The safe lit up.

Its lights flashed.

Its dial spun by itself.

And the speaking tube said, in a strangely cheerful tone:

"MIDNIGHT COMFORT SNACK PROTOCOL ACTIVATED."

Nutty's eyes bulged.

"I did not know it HAD a midnight comfort snack protocol."

Then the safe door flew open.

And the snack conveyor started moving.
