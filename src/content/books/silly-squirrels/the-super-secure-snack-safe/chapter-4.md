# Chapter 4: The Midnight Cruncher

Nobody had known there was a snack conveyor inside the safe.

Including Nutty.

Especially Nutty.

"Why is there a conveyor belt?" Tony whispered.

"Because Papa kept asking for a snack elevator," Nutty whispered back. "I told him no. I may have accidentally built a sideways one."

Papa snored again.

"Crunchy support..." he mumbled.

The safe hummed with mechanical delight.

One by one, crackers, pretzel twists, and cinnamon acorns rolled onto the conveyor and slid neatly into Papa's lap.

He did not wake up.

He simply accepted the snacks in his sleep with the calm experience of a squirrel who had apparently trained for this his entire life.

The workshop was silent for one beat.

Then Papa shifted in the chair, mumbled, "Need backup cookie," and the safe answered:

"DOUBLE COMFORT MODE ENGAGED."

The conveyor belt sped up.

"Oh no," said Nutty.

"Oh YES," said the safe.

Three cookie rolls shot out like cannonballs.

Mama caught one.

Tony caught one.

Mayor Buttersworth did not catch one.

It bounced off his sash and landed in the clue table, which collapsed into a cloud of labeled flour jars.

Mrs. Hedgehog adjusted her dusty glasses. "Well. That explains the missing snacks."

Papa, still asleep, now had a tower of crackers in his lap and a cinnamon acorn balanced on his head.

Nutty stared in disbelief. "The safe thinks Papa is an authorized nighttime emergency."

Grandma crunched another peanut. "Because he is."

Papa snored louder.

The safe horn gave a tiny toot of admiration.

Then Papa did the worst possible thing.

He smiled in his sleep and whispered, "Maximum snacks."

The safe went wild.

"MASTER COMMAND CONFIRMED!" it shouted.

Every lock popped.

The warning lights spun.

The bells clanged.

The flour cannon fired.

The confetti launcher exploded.

The emergency trumpet blared a note so heroic that it sounded like a parade had fallen down the stairs.

And the conveyor belt began flinging snacks in every direction.

Crackers flew across the workshop like tiny crunchy frisbees.

Pretzels rained from the rafters.

A whole shower of cinnamon acorns poured through the speaking tube and bounced off the floor like spicy little marbles.

"TAKE COVER!" yelled Mama, diving behind a barrel.

"THIS IS ALREADY COVERED IN FLOUR!" yelled Tony from under the clue table.

Mayor Buttersworth tried to command the situation and was immediately hit in the forehead by a very enthusiastic cheese cracker.

Outside, lights flicked on all over Nutville.

Mrs. Rabbit opened her window just in time to catch a pretzel in her teacup.

Mr. Mole looked up from his tunnel and said, "If this is another innovation, I object."

Three raccoons in the square looked around in amazement as cookies began falling from the sky.

Inside the workshop, Nutty lunged for the emergency lever.

The lever snapped off in his paw.

"That feels bad," he said.

"Try the shutdown switch!" cried Mama.

"There are six shutdown switches!"

"WHY?"

"REDUNDANCY!"

Papa stood up.

Still asleep.

Still holding a sandwich.

Still being pelted by his own snacks.

He began sleepwalking toward the safe in slow, determined steps.

The spaghetti lasers glowed.

Everyone held their breath.

Papa walked straight through them.

Nothing happened.

Nutty slapped both paws to his forehead. "I left the family setting on!"

"There is a family setting?" Tony shouted.

"Of course!"

"What does it do?"

"Mostly betrayal!"

Papa reached the open safe, hugged it gently, and said, "You understand me."

The safe answered in a warm, proud voice, "YOU ARE WELCOME, SUPREME COMMANDER."

Grandma stood up at last.

She dusted peanut skins from her apron.

She watched the flying crackers.

She watched Nutty crawling under flashing wires.

She watched Mama wrestling the trumpet.

She watched Tony get stuck in the spaghetti.

Then she sighed and said, "Children, move."

Everybody moved.

Grandma stepped calmly through the chaos and reached into her apron.

Out came the tiny stick of dynamite from the first day.

Nutty made a strangled squeak. "Grandma."

"Relax," Grandma said. "This is a polite amount."

She lit the fuse with a match, set the tiny stick beside a jammed panel on the back of the safe, and stepped away.

There was a small, tidy:

**POOF!**

A puff of soot rose into the air.

The jammed panel popped loose and landed neatly at Tony's feet.

Grandma nodded once, satisfied, and reached behind the safe.

There she found a small metal panel labeled:

**DO NOT PUT KEY HERE**

Stuck under the panel, with a bit of tape, was the plain iron key she had given them on the first day.

The workshop went still.

Nutty's jaw fell open. "I told everyone not to put the key there."

Grandma held up the key. "And naturally one of you did exactly that."

She unlocked a tiny hidden slot.

Inside was a second lever.

This one was labeled:

**REALLY ACTUALLY STOP EVERYTHING**

Grandma pulled it.

The bells stopped.

The horn died.

The lights dimmed.

The conveyor belt slowed.

One final lonely pretzel popped out, hit Papa on the nose, and bounced into Grandma's teacup.

Papa snored once more, sat back down in the chair, and kept chewing peacefully in his sleep.

The whole workshop stared at Grandma.

She sipped her pretzel tea and shrugged.

"I did mention the plain key."
