# Chapter 1: The Great Snack Emergency

Papa Nutwobble had emergency snacks.

He had rainy-day snacks, extra-rainy-day snacks, "what if lunch is late by seven minutes" snacks, and a mysterious tin labeled **DO NOT OPEN UNLESS THINGS GET EXTREMELY CRUNCHY**.

Nobody was entirely sure what "extremely crunchy" meant.

Papa was entirely sure it meant everything.

So when he opened the tin that Tuesday morning and found it EMPTY, he made a sound so dramatic that three birds fell out of a nearby tree from sheer surprise.

"NOOOOOOOOOOO!" Papa cried, clutching the empty tin to his fluffy chest. "The emergency cheese crackers are gone! The backup pretzel twists are gone! The emergency emergency cinnamon acorns are GONE!"

Nutty tumbled down from the breakfast branch with his notebook already open. "A crime?"

Mama Nutwobble zipped in from her morning run so fast that she accidentally ran one extra lap around the house before stopping. "A race?"

"Worse," Papa said in a grave whisper. "A snack robbery."

The family stared into the empty tin.

There were crumbs inside. Lots of crumbs.

Nutty adjusted his detective goggles, which were really just swimming goggles with one lens missing. "Interesting."

"What do you see?" Mama asked.

Nutty leaned closer. "Cracker dust. Pretzel salt. Cinnamon sparkles. Also..." He squinted very hard. "A suspicious amount of Papa smell."

Papa gasped. "Are you suggesting I stole my own snacks?"

"I am suggesting," Nutty said carefully, "that you were definitely near your snacks."

"I am ALWAYS near my snacks," Papa said. "That proves nothing except excellent planning."

He turned the tin upside down hopefully. One lonely peanut bounced out.

Papa stared at it. "A witness."

By the time breakfast was over, the entire town had heard the news.

Tony the Delivery Fox arrived first, still wearing one roller skate for some reason. "I came as soon as I heard about the snack tragedy."

Mrs. Hedgehog marched in carrying a clipboard. "I have organized a list of possible suspects."

"How many suspects?" Nutty asked.

"Currently?" Mrs. Hedgehog checked the clipboard. "Everyone."

Mayor Buttersworth came puffing up the hill with his ceremonial sash on backward. "Nutville will not rest until justice is served!"

"Preferably with dip," Papa muttered.

Grandma Nutwobble arrived last. She took one look at the empty tin, one look at Papa, and said, "Hmph."

Papa straightened. "What does 'hmph' mean?"

"It means either you have a thief," Grandma said, "or you had a midnight snack and forgot. Could go either way."

"I would never forget a midnight snack," Papa said with deep dignity.

Everyone considered this.

"That," said Nutty, "is actually a very good point."

He leaped onto a stump and snapped his notebook shut with a dramatic flap.

"Nutville does not need more suspicion," he declared. "Nutville needs SCIENCE."

Mama grinned. "My favorite kind of sentence."

"We need a snack container so secure, so brilliant, so impossible to crack, that no thief, squirrel, fox, hedgehog, mayor, raccoon, or sleepy family member could EVER get into it!"

Tony raised a paw. "Should the owner be able to get into it?"

Nutty paused.

"Eventually," he said.

Mrs. Hedgehog nodded thoughtfully. "Reasonable."

Papa's eyes slowly widened. "A super secure snack safe."

The words hung in the air like fireworks made of crackers.

Mama bounced in place. "With locks!"

"And alarms!" said Nutty.

"And maybe a little snack elevator inside?" Papa suggested.

"No elevator," Nutty said. "This is security, Papa, not comfort."

"What about a comfort elevator?"

"Absolutely not."

By noon, the whole plan had grown legs, wheels, bells, and at least one unnecessary trumpet.

They marched to the old mill workshop, where many of Nutville's best ideas and almost all of its worst ideas had been built.

Nutty drew a huge rectangle on the chalkboard.

"Behold!" he announced. "The Super Secure Snack Safe!"

He added labels as he spoke:

- Triple lock
- Secret code dial
- Tail-print scanner
- Anti-sneak alarm
- Crumb detector
- Backup anti-sneak alarm
- Extra backup anti-sneak alarm in case of sneaky confidence

Papa raised a paw. "Will it hold crackers, cookies, and cinnamon acorns?"

Nutty added one final note in large, serious letters:

**HOLDS MANY SNACKS**

"Perfect," Papa whispered.

Soon the workshop was buzzing.

Mama carried in an old boiler door from the mill.

Tony found a heavy metal box that had once held turnips and, according to the faded label, "possibly fireworks."

Mrs. Hedgehog contributed tiny brass locks sorted by shininess.

Mayor Buttersworth donated a red emergency lever that nobody had asked for but everybody agreed looked important.

"Excellent," the mayor said, smoothing his backward sash. "If this contraption works, perhaps Nutville can finally store the winter acorn fund somewhere safer than my desk drawer."

Mrs. Hedgehog looked up from her clipboard. "The drawer labeled `Definitely Not Town Acorns`?"

The mayor coughed. "Security through misdirection."

Grandma placed one plain iron key on the workbench.
Then she placed beside it the tiniest stick of dynamite anybody in Nutville had ever seen.

"What's that for?" Nutty asked.

"In case your fancy nonsense doesn't work," Grandma said.

"Are we putting dynamite in the snack safe?" Tony asked.

"Absolutely not," Nutty said.

"Good," said Tony.

Grandma slipped the tiny stick back into her apron. "Suit yourselves."

Nutty laughed the brave laugh of someone who has never yet been humbled by a machine he built twelve minutes ago.

"Oh, Grandma," he said. "This safe is going to be so secure it won't NEED a plain old key."

Grandma folded her paws.

"That's what they always say."

By sunset, the safe had a metal door, four locks, a turning dial, a speaking tube, and a little sign Papa painted in enormous wobbly letters:

**SUPER SECURE SNACK SAFE**

Then, underneath in smaller letters:

**NO CRUMB-FINGERED BANDITS ALLOWED**

Papa stepped back and wiped a tear from his eye. "It's beautiful."

Nutty nodded proudly. "Tomorrow, we install the security systems."

Mama cracked her knuckles. "How many security systems?"

Nutty smiled a smile that should have made everyone much more nervous than it did.

"All of them," he said.

And somewhere, very faintly, Grandma sighed the sigh of a squirrel who could already see the future and knew it was about to be hilarious.
