# Chapter 2: Maximum Security, Minimum Snacking

By the next afternoon, the Super Secure Snack Safe had stopped looking like a sturdy metal box and started looking like a nervous octopus made of locks.

Wires stuck out of the sides.

Tiny bells hung from the top.

A bicycle horn was bolted to the front.

Someone had attached two flashing lights, a walnut-shaped keypad, and a sign that read:

**WARNING: THIS SAFE IS EXTREMELY SERIOUS**

Nobody knew who wrote the sign.

Papa knew.

He was very proud of it.

Nutty stood beside the safe wearing a tool belt, a cape, and an expression of wild scientific confidence.

"Today's installation list," he said, flipping through his notebook, "includes the Tail-Print Recognition System, the Crumb-Level Alert Gauge, the Emergency Trumpet, the Flour Burst Fingerprint Cannon, and the Laser Grid."

Tony blinked. "You have actual lasers?"

"No," said Nutty. "We have spaghetti."

That was somehow worse.

Mama had spent the morning stretching uncooked noodles across the workshop doorway in neat glowing lines. Little jars of fireflies hung behind them to make them sparkle.

"Beautiful, right?" Mama said.

"Beautiful and deeply concerning," said Tony.

Mrs. Hedgehog adjusted her spectacles. "What happens if someone touches a noodle?"

Nutty pointed to a complicated lever system involving a bell, a whistle, a spring, and a bucket.

"We find out immediately."

Papa was busy loading the safe with its first official contents: two cracker boxes, a jar of cinnamon acorns, one emergency cookie roll, one extra emergency cookie roll, and one secret snack sandwich "for morale."

"There," Papa said, patting the shelf inside. "Safe, tidy, and protected."

Nutty spun the dial. "Time for the first test!"

The whole town leaned in.

"Authorized user name?" asked Nutty.

"Papa Nutwobble," Papa said grandly.

"Tail on scanner."

Papa pressed his tail against the ink pad and then slapped it onto the brass plate.

The safe made a tiny clicking noise.

Then another.

Then the speaking tube crackled and shouted in a loud, dramatic voice:

"TAIL TOO FLUFFY. ACCESS DENIED."

Everyone jumped.

Papa stared at the safe. "What?"

"Impossible," Nutty said. "You have the correct tail."

"Of course I have the correct tail."

Mama squinted at the scanner. "Maybe it wants a flatter tail?"

"I don't HAVE a flatter tail!" Papa cried. "This is my only tail!"

Nutty frowned. "Maybe the fluff setting is too strict."

He turned a little brass screw labeled **FUFFINESS**.

"That's spelled wrong," Mrs. Hedgehog said.

"Not important," Nutty replied.

"Spelling is always important."

"Later!"

They tried again.

This time the safe accepted Papa's tail, but rejected his password.

"Please enter the secret code," the safe boomed.

Papa cleared his throat. "Acorn."

"PASSWORD TOO EASY. ACCESS DENIED."

"Fine. SuperAcorn."

"PASSWORD INSUFFICIENTLY DRAMATIC."

Papa's whiskers trembled. "UltraMegaCaptainCrunchyAcornDeluxe?"

"BETTER," said the safe, "BUT STILL A LITTLE EMBARRASSING."

Mayor Buttersworth nodded. "The machine has a point."

"And if it can judge passwords this fiercely," he added, "perhaps one day it can guard Nutville's winter acorn fund."

Mrs. Hedgehog made a note on her clipboard. "Let us first see whether it can guard one cracker without humiliating the owner."

Papa took a deep breath and shouted, "SUPREME EMERGENCY SNACK COMMANDER ALPHA CRUNCH!"

The safe paused.

Then the bells rang, the lights flashed, and the horn blasted directly into Tony's ear.

"AUTHORIZED!" the safe announced.

The crowd cheered.

Papa reached for the handle.

That was when he accidentally brushed one strand of spaghetti.

Everything happened at once.

The horn honked.

The bells clanged.

The flour cannon exploded.

A hidden trapdoor dropped open under the welcome mat.

Confetti blasted from the top.

And the emergency trumpet played one long heroic note so loud that three cows across the river all sneezed in perfect harmony.

Papa disappeared in a puff of flour and landed bottom-first in a hay basket Nutty had labeled **INTRUDER CATCH ZONE**.

For one full second, nobody moved.

Then the speaking tube shouted, "INTRUDER DETAINED. NICE TRY, CRUMB-FINGERS."

Mama fell over laughing.

Tony sat down in the sawdust because his legs had stopped believing in him.

Mrs. Hedgehog calmly wrote something on her clipboard.

"What are you writing?" Nutty asked.

"Under 'observations,'" she replied, "'safe is slightly too excited.'"

Papa rose from the hay basket with confetti in his eyebrows.

"I was AUTHORIZED!"

"Technically," Nutty admitted, "you were authorized and then immediately suspicious."

"How?"

"You touched the pasta lasers."

"Because they're in front of the HANDLE!"

Nutty considered this.

"That," he said, "is excellent feedback."

For the rest of the afternoon they made improvements.

Nutty moved the spaghetti.

Mama added faster lock wheels "for efficiency."

Tony installed a softer horn, which was still very much a horn.

Mrs. Hedgehog insisted on labels for every lever.

Papa suggested a snack peephole "just so the snacks know I'm still thinking about them."

Nobody approved the peephole.

Papa cleared his throat. "Then perhaps a small bedtime standby mode? Nothing dramatic. Just something helpful in case of overnight cracker feelings."

Nutty made the face of an inventor pretending he had not heard a terrible idea.

By evening, the safe had become even more impressive and much more confusing.

Nutty finally locked the door with a dramatic spin of the dial.

"At last," he said, "the snacks are secure."

Papa folded his paws proudly. "Nothing gets in."

Grandma, who had been watching from a rocking chair with the patient expression of someone waiting for a roof to collapse, raised an eyebrow.

"Nothing gets out either," she observed.

Papa's smile vanished.

"What do you mean?"

Grandma pointed to the front of the safe.

The new label Mrs. Hedgehog had attached read:

**TO OPEN SAFE, COMPLETE ALL SEVENTEEN SECURITY STEPS IN ORDER**

Papa gulped.

"How many crackers do you think are worth seventeen steps?" Tony asked.

Papa looked at the door.

He looked at the crackers inside.

He looked back at the seventeen-step list.

"...probably two," he said quietly.

That night he slept with the safe next to the bed, just in case the crackers needed emotional support.
