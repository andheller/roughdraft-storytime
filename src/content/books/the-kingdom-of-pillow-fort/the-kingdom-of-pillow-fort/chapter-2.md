# Chapter 2: Budget Tier One: Dirt, Grass, and Ordinary Reality

This is the least glamorous option.

It is also the best one.

Suppose you buy or lease a small property. Or a campsite. Or a farm. Or a weird patch of rural ground with one shed, six trees, and a local raccoon who already behaves like customs enforcement.

Congratulations. You now possess the strongest legal starting point for a real micronation project:

private land.

Private land is powerful because it lets you do all the nation-like things that are actually fun:

- choose a name  
- create a flag  
- write a charter  
- issue memberships  
- host festivals  
- build traditions  
- assign titles  
- create a local "currency" for games, events, or internal rewards  
- make signs that say things like **WELCOME TO THE GRAND REPUBLIC OF HEDGEFORD**

What private land does **not** do is erase the state above it.

In 2026, if your land is in the United States, then state law, county rules, zoning, building codes, tax rules, environmental rules, and emergency services are still part of your life. Other countries work the same way in their own legal systems.

That sounds boring.

It is boring.

It is also why this option works.

A micronation built on normal property can become:

- a private club  
- an artist retreat  
- a historical roleplay settlement  
- a campground with lore  
- an intentional community  
- a tiny event venue with medals and nonsense

This tier is ideal for founders whose real goal is:

**I want a place, a culture, and a memorable identity.**

It is a bad tier for founders whose real goal is:

**I would like to stop paying taxes because I own a flag now.**

That founder is about to have a disappointing conversation.

Still, land has enormous advantages:

- easier access  
- easier utilities  
- easier insurance  
- easier emergency response  
- easier construction  
- better odds that your kitchen remains attached to the project

So if your budget is small, medium, or merely brave, Tier One is the smartest route.

It is not flashy.

But it lets you build a micronation people can actually visit without signing a waiver about barnacles.
