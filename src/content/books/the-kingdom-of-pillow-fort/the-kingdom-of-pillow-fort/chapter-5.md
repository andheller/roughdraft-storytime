# Chapter 5: Budget Tier Four: The Floating Metal Biscuit in the Open Sea

Now we arrive at the dream that always sounds easiest from very far away:

the offshore micronation.

Not near shore.
Not in a marina.
Not on a privately owned island.

Out there.

Beyond the ordinary.

Beyond the land.

Beyond, perhaps, good judgment.

This is the tier of seasteads, platforms, floating cities, and giant rectangular structures that look like someone dropped an apartment building onto a waffle iron.

If Tier Four has an official mascot, it is a floating metal biscuit in rough weather.

Here is the hard truth for 2026:

Going farther offshore does **not** automatically make the law go away.

Official U.S. and U.N. law-of-the-sea materials are very helpful on this point, which is disappointing for anyone hoping to out-argue the Pacific Ocean.

The broad picture is:

- coastal states have sovereignty in the territorial sea  
- coastal states have major rights and jurisdiction in the EEZ for things like artificial islands and structures  
- artificial islands do not magically count as natural islands with their own territorial sea  
- the high seas are not a place a state may simply grab as sovereign territory

This is catastrophic news for the founder whose plan was:

1. Weld platform  
2. Add flag  
3. Become nation

The sea, regrettably, demands more steps.

Such as:

- engineering  
- supply lines  
- storm survival  
- evacuation plans  
- water systems  
- power systems  
- medical planning  
- legal structure  
- insurance  
- ports that are willing to deal with your nonsense

An offshore micronation is therefore not the easiest path to freedom.

It is the hardest path to competence.

That does not mean nobody should ever attempt offshore living or offshore experimentation.

It means such a project is better understood as:

- a marine engineering venture  
- a floating development  
- a research project  
- an intentional offshore community  
- a very ambitious habitat project

It is not a cheat code.

If you have Tier Four money, you can build impressive things.

But the most likely result is not "new sovereign country."

It is "complicated floating project that still needs lawyers, engineers, safety plans, and probably a very patient accountant."
