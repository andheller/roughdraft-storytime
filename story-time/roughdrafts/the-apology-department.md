My name is Francine and I write apologies for other people, which is a job I invented because nobody else was doing it and the market was clearly huge. If you have ever been inside a school, you know that sorry is a word most kids say the way you say a password you forgot -- too fast, too quiet, and mostly wrong. I charge one snack-size bag of anything per apology, two bags if the situation involves crying, and nothing at all if you genuinely did not do it, because I am not in the business of false confessions. My desk has a sign taped to the inside that says APOLOGY DEPARTMENT -- OPEN DURING SILENT READING, which Mrs. Huang has asked me to take down four times, and I have agreed to take down four times, and it is still there because agreeing and doing are two completely different departments.
